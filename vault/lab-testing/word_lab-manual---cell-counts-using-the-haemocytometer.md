## Cell counts using the Haemocytometer


The haemocytometer is a tool that is used to count cells on a gridded background using the microscope. Traditionally this is accomplished using 40X magnification. Use care when manipulating the haemocytometer and its cover slip as they are both fragile. There are two counting areas in the haemocytometer which are mirror images of each other. Either can be used to count cells. The haemocytometer will draw exactly 10 µL into the viewing area by capillary action. Any excess sample will drain out of the gutters on the sides of the viewing area. To load the haemocytometer, ensure the cover slip is covering both view areas completely. Draw 20 µL of sample into a pipette tip and discharge the sample onto the loading area (Figure 19). Stop discharging sample once the viewing area is full.

Figure 19: The haemocytometer slide seen from above.

There is a 20 by 20 gridline counting area in the haemocytometer (Figure 20). Every 4th line is bolded to create 25 4x4 squares for counting. If the concentration of microbial cells is low in your sample, count all the cells that appear in the entire 20 by 20 gridline counting area. If the number of cells is higher, count only cells found in 5 of the 4 by 4 gridline squares and multiply your count by 5 to estimate the total number of cells in the entire counting area. When counting only 5 squares, consider counting the four corner squares and the one in the middle. Cells that are on the border of the square you are counting should only be counted if they touch the right or bottom border. Ignore cells touching the left or top boarder. Determine the concentration of cells in your sample using Equation 14.
