# word_lab manual - Setting up a lab space

> Source: Cider Institute of North America course materials

## Setting up a lab space


For most general lab tests you don’t need much space or fancy set-up. A free table-top space that has access to water and electricity is usually sufficient. Here are some other considerations when setting up your lab space:

Sometimes light or vibrations can impact your results. If possible, try to locate your lab space away from other activities that may interfere with your work

Most tests require deionized or distilled water. You can buy this water for use in your tests, or you can install a small ion-exchange filter to produce lab quality water for your use

Some tests require a source of heat. This can be with an electric heater or gas burner (Bunsen burner). There are also alcohol flame burners that can work if your space has no electricity or gas-line

Some tests require equipment that needs an electrical source. Locate equipment as close to the electrical outlet as possible to avoid cords causing obstruction hazards in your testing space. Ensure the outlet has a ground fault circuit interrupter
