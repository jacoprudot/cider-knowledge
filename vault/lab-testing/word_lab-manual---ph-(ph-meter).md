## pH (pH Meter)


pH is a measure of acid strength in juice and cider defined as the inverse log of the free hydrogen ion concentration (-log[H+]). pH is an important indication of microbial stability. Also, the amount of sulphur dioxide which is available for anti-microbial activity in juice and cider is pH dependent. pH is a logarithmic scale, which means small changes in the number of the pH represents large differences in acid strength. Because of these factors, an accurate and precise pH measurement is one of the most important pieces of analytical data a cidermaker can have.


### Equipment


pH meter

Standardized buffers at pH 4.0 and pH 7.0 (or two or three other buffers at different pHs as available)

100 mL and 250 mL beakers and flasks

Lab tissue (Kimwipe or other)

pH probe filling solution (usually Silver Chloride or Potassium Chloride)

pH electrode storage solution


### Method


Complete steps 1 to 5 only once at the start of every day.

Always rinse off the electrode with deionized H2O and dab with lab tissue before and after placing it into a solution.

Open the refilling hole (see Figure 10).

Ensure the probe is filled with internal solution.

Calibrate the pH meter once daily using fresh, unexpired, standard pH 4.0 and pH 7.0 buffers (or others). Ensure the slope of the calibration is between 95% and 105%. If the match is outside these parameters, redo the calibration. If it remains outside of these parameters, it may be time to buy a new probe.

Check the accuracy of the calibration by retesting the pH of the buffers.

Rinse and gently dab the electrode with lab tissue.

Pour approximately 30 mL of room temperature sample into a 50 mL beaker.

Immerse the pH electrode into the sample and gently move the sample to ensure the pH meter constantly receives fresh sample.

Allow the pH reading to stabilize and record the pH value.

Rinse the electrode with dH2O, dab with lab tissue, and return to the storage solution.

When finished with your measurement, close the refilling hole and place the electrode back into its storage solution.

### Troubleshooting


Don’t let electrode dry out; if it has dried out, soak in pH 4 buffer for several hours

Never touch electrode bulb

Keep electrode clean

Calibrate every day

Always use fresh pH buffers

Rinse the electrode with distilled water between measurements

Stir all buffers and samples consistently while taking measurements to ensure fresh sample is always presented to the probe

Probes eventually wear out; make sure to replace them regularly (usually every year or two)
