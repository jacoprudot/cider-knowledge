## Titratable Acidity


The organic acids present in juice and cider are weak acids, which dissociate only to a small extent. While pH measures the dissociated hydrogen ions, titratable acidity (TA) is a measure of both the dissociated and un-dissociated or partially dissociated hydrogen ions. Malic acid is the most prominent acid in apple juice and cider (it’s citric acid for pear juice and perry). For this reason, cider acidity is calculated in units of grams per litre malic acid, even though all the acids present (such as citric, succinic, and quinic, etc.) contribute to titratable acidity.


### Interferents


Carbon dioxide (CO2) from the air or produced during fermentation forms carbonic acid when dissolved in juice or cider. Carbonic acid is a titratable acid, therefore samples must be completely degassed prior to titrating to prevent interference.

Sodium hydroxide (NaOH) is used to titrate the cider samples. When stored, the concentration of NaOH declines as it reacts with CO2 from the air. Unless opening a new bottle of standardized solution, the NaOH must be standardized before use to determine its exact concentration before any titrations begin.

Note: stored NaOH will off-gas and deposit residue at the closure site. If the closure site is glass (especially glass-on-glass) this residue can permanently seal the vessel – never store NaOH in glass containers.

### Reagents


0.1 M Sodium hydroxide (NaOH) solution

Note: M = Molarity. NaOH may be sold as N = Normality. For NaOH, M = N, so they may be used interchangeably. This is not the case for all chemicals.

1% (w/v) phenolphthalein indicator

Potassium Hydrogen Phthalate (KHP)

Deionized water


### Sample Preparation


Ensure sample is free of large particulate matter, filter with a coffee filter if necessary.

Place approximately 100 ml of the sample into a 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask and degas by bubbling with air for 5 minutes.

Alternatively, degas by heating the sample to almost boiling. However, volume will be lost by water vaporization. To avoid error, the exact volume of cider/juice to be titrated is heated and then volumetrically transferred into the TA set-up.

A third option for degassing is to splash the sample back and forth between two jugs.
