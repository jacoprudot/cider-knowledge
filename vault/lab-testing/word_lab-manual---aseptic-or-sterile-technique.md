# word_lab manual - Aseptic or Sterile technique

> Source: Cider Institute of North America course materials

## Aseptic or Sterile technique


Aseptic or sterile technique is a collection of practices designed to ensure a sterile work environment and minimize the chance of microbial contamination.


#### PREPARATION


Wipe down the benchtop and all equipment with 70% ethanol. Allow to air dry.


#### GLOVES


Always wear gloves to maintain sterility. A double glove system is recommended. The first pair of gloves remain on your hands. The second pair of gloves are removed in between techniques to prevent cross-contamination. A fresh set of gloves is then applied before moving onto the next task.


#### ZONE OF STERILITY


Create a work zone that maintains a sterile state by lighting a Bunsen burner and working within the updraft caused by the heat of the flame. This prevents contaminants from settling on your work (usually an approx. 30 cm diameter zone around the base of the burner).
