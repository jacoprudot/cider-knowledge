# word_lab manual - Alcohol

> Source: Cider Institute of North America course materials

## Alcohol


Distillation followed by hydrometry can be used to determine the alcohol content of cider. The sample is distilled and the distillate is brought up to the same volume as the original sample and measured with an alcohol hydrometer. The starting temperature of the cider and the measuring temperature of the distillate must all be the same, preferably the temperature at which the hydrometers are calibrated (hydrometry measurements are temperature dependent). Since alcohol measurements are concentration measurements, the volume of distillate must equal the volume of the initial sample.


### Interferents


Interference can occur from high concentrations of acetic acid and sulphur dioxide, as both are distillable. They can interfere by lowering the apparent alcohol content in the cider. Ciders with high levels of either should be neutralized with 2.0 N NaOH before distillation.

### Collection and Measurement of Distillate


Bring about 300 mL of sample to 20°C.

Rinse a 250 mL volumetric flask with a few mL of the 20°C cider, discard and then accurately measure 250 mL of cider into the volumetric flask.

Volumetrically transfer contents to the round bottom distillation flask.

Add 50 mL of dH2O to the distillation flask to prevent charring.

Add a few boiling chips to the flask.

If newly fermented ciders are being tested, add a drop of Tween to prevent excessive foaming.

Position and connect the distillation flask, ensuring a tight seal between the expansion bulb, flask and condenser.

Turn on the condenser water.

Place approximately 7 mL of dH2O into the bottom of the receiving flask prior to collecting the distillate.

Make sure the rubber tubing from the condenser is placed into the bottom of the flask, under the seed water.

Surround the 250 mL volumetric collection flask with an ice bath.

Bring the sample to a moderate boil.

Collect approximately 200 mL of distillate.

Volumetrically transfer the distillate into the same 250 mL volumetric flask used initially to measure the sample.

Invert and mix and bring the sample temperature to 20°C in the water bath.

Bring the sample to volume with dH2O.

Transfer distillate to a hydrometer cylinder and immerse the alcohol hydrometer into the liquid.

Once the hydrometer has stabilized, record the alcohol content.
